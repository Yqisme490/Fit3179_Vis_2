Data Science Jobs in Australia

Harvard Business Review calls the role of a data scientist as "the sexiest job of the 21st century". In 2022, data science ranked in the top five most in-demand and highest paying jobs in Australia. Its annual average growth rate is predicted to be 2.4 per cent, according to Deloitte, a whole percentage point higher than the average job rate growth.

The bar chart shows the top 10 most in-demand data science jobs in Australia. From the bar chart above, we can see that three most in-demand data science jobs are Data Scientist, Data Analyst and Data Engineer.

In order to pursue a successful career as a data scientist, professionals must master different technical skills. The bar chart shows the most important skills for getting a data science job. From the bar chart above, we can see that three most important skills are SQL, Python and R. The least important skills are Matlab, NoSql and Julia.

The map visualization above shows the average base salary for top 5 data science jobs in different states of Australia. The lighter the color, the lower the value; the darker the color, the higher the value. Of all the other states, New South Wales has the highest average estimated base salary at $111,057 and Victoria has the lowest average estimated base salary at $94,633.

The boxplot above shows the distribution of base salaries for different industries (Top 10). From the boxplot above, we can see that Internet & Web Services industry has the highest median base salary at $183,000 and HR consulting industry has the lowest median base salary at $183,000. Business Consulting industry has the highest box length, which means that the estimated base salary in this industry is widely dispersed.

In the Diverging Stacked Bar Chart above, we can see a comparison between private and public companies based on different criteria. Those criteria include Company Rating, Career Opportunities, Senior Management, Compensation and Benefits, Work Life Balance and Culture and Values. We can see that private companies have relatively higher average values than public companies in each of these five criteria. Therefore, we can conclude that private companies are more desirable for data scientists in Australia.
